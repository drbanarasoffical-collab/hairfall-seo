What Is Hair Fall?

Hair fall is one of the most common concerns affecting both men and women. Losing a small amount of hair every day is normal, but excessive hair loss may indicate an underlying issue that deserves attention.

Hair growth follows a natural cycle. When this cycle becomes disturbed, hair shedding may increase and hair density may gradually decrease.

Common Causes of Hair Fall

  • Genetic factors
  • Stress and emotional strain
  • Nutritional deficiencies
  • Hormonal changes
  • Scalp disorders
  • Certain medications
  • Poor hair care practices

Identifying the underlying cause is important because treatment approaches may differ depending on the reason for hair loss.

Symptoms of Excessive Hair Loss

  • Increased shedding during washing or combing
  • Thinning of hair
  • Widening hair part
  • Receding hairline
  • Bald patches
  • Reduced hair volume

Treatment Options

Treatment depends on the cause of hair fall. Maintaining a balanced diet, managing stress, improving scalp health, and seeking professional guidance may help support healthy hair growth.

People experiencing persistent hair loss should consider medical evaluation to identify possible nutritional deficiencies, hormonal imbalances, or other health conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my hair falling out?

Hair fall can result from stress, genetics, hormonal changes, nutritional deficiencies, or certain medical conditions.

Can lost hair grow back?

In many cases, hair regrowth is possible when the underlying cause is identified and addressed appropriately.

What vitamins help healthy hair growth?

Iron, Vitamin D, Zinc, Biotin, and Vitamin B12 may play important roles in hair health when deficiencies are present.
